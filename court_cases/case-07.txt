People v. James Turner

Charge: Second-Degree Murder
Summary:
Background: James Turner was accused of murdering a rival gang member during a drive-by shooting. The prosecution argued that Turner sought revenge after a previous altercation.
Legal Arguments: The prosecution presented ballistics evidence linking Turner to the weapon used in the crime and surveillance footage showing his vehicle near the scene. The defense argued mistaken identity, claiming Turner was not present during the shooting.
Witness Statements: Gang members testified under immunity, describing the ongoing feud and Turner’s involvement. A ballistics expert confirmed that the bullets recovered matched Turner’s firearm.
Verdict: Turner was found guilty of second-degree murder and sentenced to 25 years to life in prison.
